Why Aunt Jane From The Five Nights At Freddy’s Movie Looks So Familiar

If you find yourself experiencing a sense of déjà vu when watching the Five Nights at Freddy’s movie and wondering why Aunt Jane looks so familiar, you’re not alone. The actress behind the character, Mary Stuart Masterson, has had a remarkable career in the film industry, leaving a lasting impact with her talent and versatility.

Mary Stuart Masterson’s Acting Career

Mary Stuart Masterson’s journey in the world of acting began with her debut in the 1975 film “The Stepford Wives.” From there, she quickly established herself as a force to be reckoned with, landing significant roles in notable films. However, it was her major breakthrough in the 1987 romantic drama “Some Kind of Wonderful” that truly solidified her status as a rising star.

Early Roles and Debut

Masterson’s early roles showcased her exceptional talent and set the stage for her future success. In the 1986 crime drama “At Close Range,” she shared the screen with acclaimed actors such as Sean Penn and Christopher Walken, delivering exceptional performances that captivated audiences.

Major Breakthrough in “Some Kind of Wonderful”

“Some Kind of Wonderful” served as a turning point in Masterson’s career, propelling her into the spotlight. This classic John Hughes film, with its compelling love triangle between Keith, Watts, and Amanda, resonated with audiences and showcased Masterson’s ability to bring depth and authenticity to her performances.

“Some Kind of Wonderful”

“Some Kind of Wonderful” is a captivating film that explores the complexities of love and friendship. The heart of the story lies in its intricate love triangle, following the journey of Keith, Watts, and Amanda as they navigate their emotions and undergo personal growth.

Love Triangle and Character Development

Watts, portrayed by Mary Stuart Masterson, undergoes a remarkable character development throughout the film. Initially portrayed as a tomboyish friend to Keith, she evolves into a complex and multidimensional character, grappling with her own desires and insecurities. Masterson’s nuanced portrayal captures the essence of a young woman on a journey of self-discovery.

Changes to the Character and Name

During the production of “Some Kind of Wonderful,” the filmmakers made significant changes to Watts’ character, resulting in a more inclusive and relatable portrayal. Originally written as a tomboy named Keith, the character underwent a transformation to become more gender-neutral. This decision added depth to the story and allowed for a broader range of audience connection. The name change from Keith to Watts, inspired by the Rolling Stones’ drummer Charlie Watts, gave the character a unique and memorable identity.

Masterson’s Commitment to the Role

Mary Stuart Masterson’s dedication to her craft is evident in her commitment to bringing authenticity to her roles. In “Some Kind of Wonderful,” her portrayal of Watts goes beyond just delivering lines and embodying a character. She fully immerses herself in the role, leaving a lasting impression on both the audience and her fellow cast members.

Learning to Play Drums

One of the remarkable aspects of Masterson’s performance in “Some Kind of Wonderful” is her ability to play the drums. To accurately portray Watts, Masterson took on the challenge of learning this instrument, investing time and effort into mastering the art of drumming. Her dedication not only added an extra layer of authenticity to the character but also showcased Masterson’s commitment to her craft.

In conclusion, Mary Stuart Masterson’s portrayal of Watts in “Some Kind of Wonderful” showcased her talent and challenged traditional gender roles. Her commitment to the role, including learning to play the drums, further solidified her success and left a lasting impact on the film industry.
