Can you name an orchestra with which he has performed?

What is one of his notable recordings?

One of his notable recordings includes the first stereo recording of Sir Arthur Bliss’s Piano Concerto in B-flat with the Philharmonia Orchestra under Sir Malcolm Sargent.

Has he contributed to music education?

Yes, he has been an examiner for the Australian Music Examinations Board and has adjudicated various competitions, including the ABC Symphony Australia Young Performers Awards.

Did he ever live in the United States?

Yes, between 1967 and 1972, he lived in the U.S., where he was a faculty member of the New England Conservatory in Boston.

What role did he play in Australian music education?

In Australia, he served as a piano tutor at Monash University and taught full-time at several educational institutions until his retirement in 2003.

Yes, composers such as Felix Werder, Richard St. Clair, Geoffrey Allen, and Michael Bertram have written works especially for him.
