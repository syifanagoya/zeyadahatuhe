Early Life

On September 21, 1979, Otis and Debra Burns, residents of Anderson, South Carolina; a male child was born, whom they christened as John Marks Burns. Mark Burns took to the keyboards at an early age and began playing music and singing. He performed with his family group and sang gospel music. Their family group was called Burns Brothers of Belton, South Carolina. They traveled all over the country and performed at religious gatherings.

His dedication brought him success in 1995 when he was just 16 years old. He was licensed as a minister of the gospel under his father. This was at the Mt. Pleasant Missionary Baptist Church in Pelzer, South Carolina.

Along with his religious duties, he also attended the Belton-Honea Path High School. In 1998 he received a South Carolina GED from the South Carolina Department of Education. Then he joined the North Greenville University for one semester.

In 2005, Burns was awarded the priesthood at Traveler’s Rest Baptist Church in Seneca, South Carolina.

Career

In 2015, Burns and his wife Tomarra Burns, founded the NOW Television Network, in South Carolina. It is a Christian television network. He also began hosting the show Lift Your Voice Show, for which he is also the executive producer.

He is a Trump supporter and traveled extensively with the Trump campaign. He was the person who spoke before Trump and introduced him to the crowd of supporters.

He also appeared on several news channels on behalf of Presidential candidate Trump and his campaign. These TV channels include CNN, BBC, FOX News, MSNBC, CBS News, Al Jazeera-America, National Public Radio, and many more.

He also landed himself on many unintended controversies. Burns performed the benediction prayers during Republican conventions. He called Trump a ‘man of God’ and labeled Hillary Clinton and the Democratic Party as the ‘enemy.’

He re-tweeted a fake image of Hillary Clinton in blackface. He was criticized for inserting God and religion into politics. Later he apologized for using inappropriate words.

Burns also claimed that he has a Bachelor of Science degree from North Greenville University; that he was a member of the Kappa Alpha Psi; and that he has served six years in the U.S. Army Reserve. All these claims were found to be false in an investigation by the TV channel CNN and the media. He later apologized for these exaggerations in his biography.
